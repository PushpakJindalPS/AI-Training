Welcome everyone. Before we start, we'll cover why we're here, what we'll cover across these workshops, and what you'll walk away with.

Why Are We Here?

Everyone knows AI exists. Fewer know how to integrate it best in their workflows.

Where We Are
  • AI tools are everywhere — in your inbox, browser, and productivity suite
  • Very few have a complete, joined-up picture of what it is and how to use it deliberately
  • Trying to figure out how to use it effectively
  • The result: inconsistent results, missed opportunities, and uncertainty about when to trust it
Where we want to be
  • Understand what the technology actually is — not just the surface
  • Know why prompts work or don't work
  • Understand what agents are and how they can work for you
  • Be able to build simple AI workflows — without writing a single line of code
Don't spend long here — 2 minutes max. The goal is to validate that the gap is real and that this workshop is the right response to it. Avoid being preachy. Most people in the room will nod along — this framing is just permission to say "yes, this is actually useful."

What This Workshop Is

Concepts first, practical throughout.

What it is
  • Concepts-first — every session builds understanding before asking you to do anything
  • Platform-agnostic — principles that apply to any AI tool, not one product
  • Progressive — each workshop builds on the last; by the end you'll have a holistic understanding of using AI effectively
What it is not
  • Not a coding exercise — everything is no-code or minimal-code
  • Not about completely automating your tasks — it's about making your job easier and delivery times shorter
  • Not a deep technical course — we'll touch on underlying concepts only enough to understand AI behaviour
  • Not a one-tool tutorial — concepts work across ChatGPT, Claude, Copilot, and beyond
The "not a coding course" point relieves a lot of anxiety in the room. Say it clearly and mean it. Session 4 involves building in n8n — completely visual, no code. Reassure anyone who looks nervous.

The live demo point is important to call out. A lot of workshops talk about AI — we're going to show it. Set the expectation that the demos are unscripted — sometimes they go slightly differently than planned, and that's fine. Real AI behaviour, warts and all, is more educational than a polished recording.

What to do with the answers: Q1 — most hands up → "That's exactly the gap we're here to close." Q2 — lots of hands → "By Session 2 you'll understand exactly why that happened." Q3 — any hands → "Perfect — you'll leave with a clearer picture than people who've been guessing." This icebreaker warms the room up and gives you signal on where to pitch the next four sessions. If the room is more experienced than expected, move faster through the conceptual slides in Session 1. If they're newer, slow down on the fundamentals. Calibrate based on what you see here.
